Best information about this park

Menagesha Suba Forest Park, located near Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, is dubbed the oldest park in Africa. It has been a state forest since the 15th century AD. Emperor Zere Yacob is credited with protecting this fascinating ecosystem during his reign. Within the forest, you can explore either the tangled native forest itself or hike one of the peaks of an ancient volcano. The view from the top is magnificent. There are at least three camp sites where you can camp, make a fire, barbecue, and relax. The forest is dense and atmospheric, with native vegetation. While exploring, you might encounter endemic birds like the Ayssinian Oriole and White-backed Black Tit. Additionally, black and white colobus monkeys inhabit the area, adding to its natural charm. Various tour options allow you to experience Menagesha Suba Forest Park. Consider day trips, private hiking tours, or multi-day adventures to fully appreciate this unique natural treasure. Despite some challenges with certain tour companies, I recommend visiting Menagesha Suba Forest Park.
